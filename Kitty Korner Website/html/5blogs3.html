Here are some tips on How to Make Your Home More Cat-Friendly:


Cats are curious and playful creatures, and they love to explore their surroundings. However, not all homes are created equal when it comes to cat safety and comfort. If you're thinking about bringing a cat into your home, there are a few things you can do to make it more cat-friendly.


  • Provide plenty of hiding places. Cats love to have places to hide and feel safe, so make sure to provide them with plenty of options. This could include things like cat trees, boxes, or even just a pile of blankets.
  • Keep your home clean and free of hazards. Cats are very agile, but they can still get hurt if they trip over something or get into something they shouldn't. Make sure to sweep and mop regularly, and put away any potential hazards, such as cleaning supplies, medications, or small objects that could be choking hazards.
  • Provide plenty of scratching posts. Cats scratch to mark their territory and to keep their claws in good condition. Make sure to provide them with plenty of scratching posts, both indoors and outdoors.
  • Give your cat plenty of places to climb. Cats love to climb, so give them plenty of opportunities to do so. This could include things like cat trees, shelves, or even just a sturdy piece of furniture.
  • Keep your cat's litter box clean. Cats are very particular about their litter boxes, so make sure to keep them clean on a regular basis. A dirty litter box can lead to litter box avoidance, which can be a sign of a medical problem.
  • Provide your cat with plenty of toys. Cats need to play to stay healthy and happy. Make sure to provide them with plenty of toys, both interactive and non-interactive.
  • Spend time with your cat. Cats are social creatures, and they need interaction with their humans. Make sure to spend time with your cat every day, playing with them, petting them, and talking to them.

By following these tips, you can make your home a safe and comfortable place for your cat.
